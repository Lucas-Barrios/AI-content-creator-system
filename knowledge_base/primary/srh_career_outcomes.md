## 1. Graduate Role Targets

SRH positions its graduates — especially EMBA — for senior and executive roles. These are explicitly named on the site, not inferred.

### C-Suite and Executive Leadership
- Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
- Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
- General Manager
- Founder / Managing Director

### Senior Functional Leadership
- Head of Department
- Country Division Head
- Finance Director
- Marketing Director
- HR Director
- Head of Strategic Development
- Head of Business Development

### Advisory and Consulting
- Management Consultant
- Project Manager

### Entrepreneurial Path
- SRH has operated the **Founder Institute** since 2010, supporting students, graduates (up to 5 years post-graduation), and staff in launching ventures
- The Institute operates across three levels: **teaching, mentoring, and networking**
- Support includes idea validation, co-working space, investor matching, and access to startup ecosystem partners
- SRH achieved a **top position in the EXIST start-up grant programme** (March 2026) — a federal government scheme funding university-born startups
- Example startups from the Founder Institute: Appose (HR algorithms), Living Brain (VR rehabilitation), Paicon (health in the cloud), NextX (recruitment platform), Compleat (fitness and nutrition)

## 2. Career Support Infrastructure

SRH has a dedicated Career Service & Development team embedded across campuses — not a one-size-fits-all resource but a personalised, named-contact model.

### Career Service & Development Team
- Individual career coaching and consulting
- Application document review (CV, cover letter)
- Interview preparation and simulation
- Internship and job search support
- Workshop programme: **Career:Skills** — covering professional, digital, and personal competencies
- Dedicated contacts per campus (Berlin, Hamburg, Leipzig, Gera, Heidelberg, and more)

### Career:Skills Workshop Programme
Workshops are designed to build three competency areas during study, not after:
- **Professional skills** — application process, career planning, industry knowledge
- **Digital skills** — practical digital tools and workplace technology literacy
- **Personal skills** — self-presentation, communication, confidence

### JobTeaser Partnership
- SRH runs the **SRH Career Center by JobTeaser** — a dedicated career platform for students and alumni
- Companies can post internships, working student roles, and entry-level positions directly to the SRH student community
- Available to corporate partners free of charge, incentivising employer engagement
- URL: srh-university.jobteaser.com

### Alumni Network
- Active alumni network used as a career resource
- Graduates are connected to the network from the start of their studies, not at graduation
- Used for mentoring, referrals, and job leads

## 3. Corporate Partnerships and Industry Access

SRH explicitly frames corporate partnerships as a structural feature — students enter real projects with real companies as part of their studies.

### Partnership Models
- **Dual partner companies** — companies co-designing programmes with SRH for Study & Work students
- **Internship providers** — companies hosting students during degree
- **Project partners** — companies commissioning live projects from student cohorts
- **Partner universities** — exchange programme network for intercultural development

### Named Strategic Partners (2025–2026)
- **SAP** — strategic partnership extended to India (March 2026); digital business and ERP expertise directly accessible
- **Accor** — SRH became Preferred Partner for dual study programmes in Germany (April 2026); hospitality sector talent pipeline
- **Green Industry Cluster** — sustainability and green economy sector membership

### What This Means for Students
- Corporate contacts are built before graduation, not after
- Real-world project experience counts toward the degree
- Early employer relationships increase direct hiring likelihood
- Industry exposure spans tech, hospitality, sustainability, healthcare, and more

## 6. Scholarships Supporting Career Accessibility

SRH offers two scholarship routes, relevant for content targeting cost-conscious prospective students:

### SRH Scholarship (own funds)
A full scholarship covering tuition fees for the full standard study period. Six categories:
- Students with disabilities
- Young entrepreneurs
- Exceptional social commitment
- Master's students
- Students with families
- Talented Bachelor applicants

### Germany Scholarship (Deutschlandstipendium)
- Federal initiative co-funded 50/50 by private sponsors and government
- €300/month non-repayable grant
- Available to exceptionally talented students
