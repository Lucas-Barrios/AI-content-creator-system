## 1. Institutional Overview

SRH University (German: *SRH Hochschule*) is one of Germany's leading private universities of applied sciences. It is part of **SRH Holding**, a non-profit foundation operating across education and healthcare, headquartered in Heidelberg.

**Key facts:**
- **Status:** State-recognised private university (first recognised by the Baden-Württemberg Ministry of Science, Research and Arts in 1972)
- **Network size:** Part of the SRH University Network — 7 to 8 state-recognised universities under the SRH umbrella, depending on counting methodology
- **Campuses:** 18 campus locations across Germany, including Heidelberg, Berlin, Hamburg, Munich, Dresden, Leipzig, Stuttgart, Hamm, Fürth, Cologne, Riedlingen, Gera, North Rhine-Westphalia
- **Programs offered:** Approximately 196 study programmes across the SRH University network; up to 340+ when counting all SRH Universities Network institutions
- **Students:** 9,500+ enrolled across the network
- **International students:** Drawn from over 140 countries; 100+ countries represented at the university
- **English-taught programs:** 100+ in the English-taught portfolio
- **Modes of study:** On-campus, online, hybrid, study-abroad, and from-abroad options

**Type of institution:** University of applied sciences (*Hochschule für Angewandte Wissenschaften* / *HAW*). This means SRH is **practice-oriented rather than research-led**. As a consequence, SRH does not appear prominently in research-focused global rankings such as QS World University Rankings or Times Higher Education — a fact the institution does not hide and which shapes its positioning around employability and applied learning.

**Major awards and recognition:**
- Genius Loci Award for Excellence in Teaching (2018), awarded by Stifterverband and the Volkswagen Foundation
- "Top Distance Learning University" seal from FernstudiumCheck (2025), recommendation rate of 99% for SRH Distance Learning University (Riedlingen)
- The SRH Distance Learning University is the largest private university in Baden-Württemberg

## 2. The CORE Principle (Pedagogical Model)

**Critical for content generation:** The CORE principle is SRH's **single most distinctive institutional feature** and should be referenced in nearly any program-related content. It is the institution's genuine differentiator versus traditional German universities and most private competitors.

**CORE = Competence Oriented Research and Education**

CORE is a complete restructuring of the traditional German university model, designed and implemented by SRH. It is not a marketing slogan — it is the actual operating model for teaching across all SRH campuses.

**How CORE works in practice:**

- **5-week blocks:** The academic year is divided into eight 5-week blocks. Students focus on one or two subjects at a time rather than juggling many parallel courses.
- **Maximum 8 ECTS per block:** This pacing keeps cognitive load manageable and allows deep focus.
- **Block-end exams:** Examinations take place at the end of each 5-week block, not in a stressful exam season.
- **Small group sizes:** Teaching is delivered in small groups, with experienced lecturers and dedicated study coaches.
- **Hands-on, project-based learning:** Theory is immediately applied. Students work on real cases, often with corporate partners.
- **Personal coaching:** Each student is supported by a study coach who helps with personal goals, study planning, and career orientation — not just academic content.

**Why this matters for content:** When competitors (TUM, LMU, public German universities, other private institutions) talk about teaching, they typically reference traditional lecture/seminar formats, semesters, and standard exam periods. SRH's CORE principle is **structurally different**, and saying so concretely (5-week blocks, 8 ECTS cap, block-end exams, study coach) is far more credible than vague claims about "innovative teaching."

## 3. Schools and Fields of Study

SRH University organises its program portfolio around the following fields:

- **Arts, Design & Music** — Advertising & Brand Design, Film & Motion Design, Illustration, Photography, Music Production, Audio Design, Web Development, Communication
- **Business & Law** — including the LL.M. portfolio
- **Communication, Media & Marketing**
- **Computer & Data Science** — including Big Data, AI, Applied Computer Science
- **Engineering, Sustainability & Architecture**
- **Health, Education & Social Work**
- **Hospitality**
- **Psychology & Therapy** — most health-related fields are taught in German due to professional licensing requirements (notable English exceptions: Dance Movement Therapy, Music Therapy)
- **Supply Chain Management**

**Degree levels offered:** Bachelor's, Master's, MBA, LL.M., DBA (Doctorate in Business Administration, in collaboration with the University of Liverpool), university certificates, foundation-year programs (via SRH International College).

**Languages of instruction:** German and English. The English portfolio is broad in business, engineering, IT, design, and creative fields. Health, psychology and most therapy programs are predominantly German-taught due to clinical licensing.

### 4.1 MSc Applied Data Science and Artificial Intelligence

**Level:** Master of Science
**School:** School of Arts, Information and Media (Heidelberg) / Computer Science portfolio
**Duration:** 4 semesters / 2 years
**Language of instruction:** English
**Locations:** Fürth, Hamburg, Heidelberg, Munich
**ECTS:** 120

**Tuition (indicative):**
- EU/EEA students: from €830/month
- Non-EU/EEA students: €6,800–€6,949 per semester (varies by source year)
- One-off enrolment fee: €750 (EU/EEA) / €1,000 (non-EU/EEA)

**Curriculum highlights:**

*Semester 1 — Foundations:*
- First Steps to Case Studies
- Data Analytics I
- Data Engineering I
- Data Management I
- Data Storytelling & Communication I
- Big Data Programming I

*Semester 2 — Deepening:*
- Case Study I
- Data Analytics II
- Data Engineering II
- Data Storytelling & Communication II
- Privacy, Ethics, and International Law

*Semester 3 — Specialisation:*
- Cloud Computing and Data Architectures
- Data Visualisation and Storytelling
- Deep Learning
- AI Vision
- Entrepreneurship
- 6-month internship OR semester abroad

*Semester 4 — Master's Thesis:*
- Master's Thesis (research- or hands-on-based)
- Master Thesis Seminar

**Heidelberg specialisation — comprehensive Data Science & Intelligent Interactive Systems:** Heidelberg offers extended courses including Advanced Python for Data Science, AI Vision, Media Analytics & AI-Driven Content Systems, Generative AI Applications, and Collaborative Industry Projects. This is positioned as the most comprehensive data science environment within the program.

**What students learn:**
- Mathematical, statistical, analytical, and machine learning / deep learning techniques
- Working with large and often unstructured data sets
- Machine learning, data engineering, natural language processing, generative AI, cloud computing
- Ethical AI principles applied across diverse domains

**Admission requirements:**
- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Information Systems, or related field (180 ECTS)
- For some variants: at least one year of qualified, subject-relevant professional experience in IT, data science, data analytics, marketing, logistics, production, or finance
- English proficiency proof for non-native speakers (TOEFL / IELTS / Duolingo / PTE)

**Career outcomes — graduate employers cited by SRH:**
ABB, Heidelberg Cement, Airbus, Accenture, Adidas, Audi, Bosch, BMW, BASF, Deloitte, Deutsche Börse, DKFZ, PWC, SAP, Verivox, plus successful SMEs and public-sector roles. Doctoral studies eligibility upon completion.

### 4.4 M.Eng. International Business and Engineering

**Level:** Master of Engineering
**School:** Engineering, Management, Sustainability and Industrial Engineering portfolio
**Duration:** 3–4 semesters
**Language of instruction:** English
**Location:** Heidelberg

**Positioning:** Sits at the intersection of technology, management, and sustainability. Designed for the interface role between technical and commercial development.

**Curriculum highlights:**
- Strategic decision-making, HR management, managerial tasks
- Sustainability assessment: energy-related, financial, ecological, and social challenges
- SAP applications (with certifications), AI-related tools, Autodesk Inventor
- International legal frameworks and intercultural competencies

**Distinctive coursework example (verbatim from SRH):** Students collaboratively develop sustainable and innovative product concepts using extensive database resources, complemented by access to other local academic libraries (including Heidelberg University's system). Students learn to:
- Identify needs and define product specifications using research insights, AI-supported analysis, and industry data
- Evaluate feasibility through surveys, interviews, and professional networking
- Analyse environmental and social impacts with IoT sensors, simulation software, and AI-enhanced assessment tools
- Outline business models, occasionally supported by 3D-printed prototypes
- Present results in a consulting-oriented format using Think-Cell

**Admission requirements:**
- Bachelor's degree (final grade ≥ 2.5 in German system) in business engineering, engineering, natural sciences, economics, finance, business administration, or computer science
- Applicants with non-standard qualifications or relevant professional experience considered case-by-case
- Preliminary engineering course available for graduates with economics/business administration backgrounds

**Career profile:** Confident management roles in technical and business development, both domestic and international, with sustainability and value-creation framing.

**Doctoral pathway:** Eligibility for doctoral studies upon successful completion.

## 5. Pre-Master's and Foundation Pathways (SRH International College)

For applicants who do not directly meet entry requirements, SRH operates the **SRH International College**, a foundation-year and pre-master's pathway provider.

**Pre-Master's tracks:**
- Business
- Engineering / IT
- Creative Studies

**What pre-master's includes:**
- Theoretical and practical concepts in chosen field
- Analytical, technical, and interpersonal skills development
- English language and academic skills enhancement
- German language modules
- Postgraduate research and ethics modules
- Module on understanding German higher education's institutional culture, practices, norms, and expectations in an international academic context

**Pathway benefit:** Successful completion leads directly into the chosen Master's program at SRH University without re-applying.

## 6. Tuition, Fees, and Financing

**General range (Bachelor's, indicative annual):** approximately USD 8,000 – USD 11,500 per year, depending on program, duration, and campus location.

**Master's tuition (indicative):** ranges from €790–€830 per month for EU/EEA students up to €6,450–€6,949 per semester for non-EU/EEA students, depending on the program.

**One-off enrolment fee:** €750 (EU/EEA) / €1,000 (non-EU/EEA), paid once at start of studies.

**Fee stability promise:** Monthly/semester tuition fees remain the same for the entire duration of the study programme.

**Why SRH charges tuition (institutional positioning):** "As a state-recognised private university, SRH University is financed by tuition fees. The fees go towards equipping our campuses, providing services for our students, and ensuring quality assurance."

**Scholarship offering:**
- Merit-based scholarships ranging from **10% to 50% of first-year tuition**
- Awarded through holistic review of academic performance, motivation, extracurricular engagement, and overall student profile
- Additional external scholarship opportunities (DAAD, Leadership for Africa, etc.)

## 7. Admissions Framework

**General application process:**
1. Submit online application via the SRH application portal (rolling admissions for most programs)
2. Document review
3. Personal or phone interview (if requirements are met)
4. Acceptance letter and study contract issued
5. For non-EU/EEA students: visa application using the admission letter
6. Formal enrolment, fee payment, document submission, class registration
7. Recommended arrival a few weeks before program start
8. Orientation programme participation

**For US applicants to Bachelor's programs (typical):**
- High school transcripts and diploma
- English language proficiency proof (waived for native English speakers)
- Personal statement / motivation letter
- Optional: standardised test scores

**For Master's applicants:**
- Bachelor's degree (program-specific field requirements)
- Minimum GPA varies by program (commonly final grade ≥ 2.5 in German system)
- Language proficiency proof (English: TOEFL / IELTS / Duolingo / PTE, accepted minimums vary by program)
- Some programs require relevant professional experience

**For PhD applicants:**
- Relevant Master's degree or equivalent
- Detailed research proposal
- Academic references (1–2)
- Language proficiency proof

## 9. Campus and Living

**Heidelberg campus (flagship):**
- Located in Heidelberg-Wieblingen, west of Heidelberg on the left bank of the Neckar
- Approximately 596 one-bedroom flats available for students and trainees at favourable rental conditions; total housing expansion targeting close to 1,000 rooms
- Monthly housing costs: under €500 per month for SRH-managed housing (one of the most affordable options in the region; high demand)
- Library on campus with 38 reading and work stations, 8 group rooms with Wi-Fi, 22 PC workstations, lounge-café
- The Cube: campus canteen
- SRH Campus Sports e.V.: sports and recreational activities for all ages and fitness levels
- Network of 80+ international partner universities; ERASMUS+ participation

**Other major campuses include:** Berlin (technology and management focus, formerly OTA Hochschule, founded 2002, ~2,200+ students), Hamburg, Dresden, Munich, Hamm (home of Germany's first Bachelor's degree programme in logistics), Leipzig, Stuttgart, Fürth, Cologne.

## 11. Distinctive Institutional Claims (For Reference in Content)

When generating content, the following are factually defensible claims that distinguish SRH from generic German higher education:

1. **CORE principle is real and structurally different** — 5-week blocks, 8 ECTS cap per block, block-end exams, study coaches, small groups
2. **One of Germany's most extensive program ranges** — 196+ programs at SRH University, 340+ across the SRH Universities Network
3. **Practice-orientation is institutional, not aspirational** — embedded internships, corporate partnerships baked into curricula (e.g., SAP, Siemens, Autodesk certifications)
4. **Genuine geographic diversity** — 18 campuses, allowing program/location combinations
5. **State recognition is permanent** — distinguishes SRH from many private competitors
6. **Award-winning teaching** — Genius Loci Award (2018) is verifiable
7. **International scale** — 9,500+ students, 100+ countries represented, 100+ English-taught programs
8. **Foundation-year safety net** — pre-master's and pre-bachelor's pathways via SRH International College reduce risk for applicants

## 12. Things to Avoid Saying in Content

The following are common claims SRH **does not** make and should not appear in content:

- ❌ "World-class research university" — SRH is a *university of applied sciences*, not research-led; this would be both inaccurate and contrary to its actual positioning
- ❌ Comparisons to QS or Times Higher Education rankings — SRH does not compete on these axes; mentioning them weakens positioning
- ❌ Vague claims about "innovation" without naming the CORE principle — generic
- ❌ "World-class," "cutting-edge," "unparalleled" — generic AI-language SRH avoids in its own materials
- ❌ Implying free tuition — SRH is private and tuition-funded; transparency about this is part of its positioning
