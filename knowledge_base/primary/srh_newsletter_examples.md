### Brand & SEO Notes — Newsletter 1

**Voice:** Peer-to-peer, not institutional. The opening question ("Do you remember...") mirrors SRH's brand language of putting the reader at the centre of the story. It avoids corporate fundraising clichés ("Your generous contribution will help...") in favour of narrative and specificity.

**Tone dial:** Warm and conversational — sitting at the "More conversational" end of the tone dial for this audience and goal. Alumni relationships are earned; this email respects that.

**Messaging pillars used:** Career transformation (referencing the alumni's own journey), community (the SRH network extending across generations).

**Concrete impact amounts:** Specific gift levels (€50 / €150 / €500) replace vague calls to "donate." Specificity reduces decision friction and signals transparency — an E-E-A-T trust signal.

**Single CTA:** One action: donate. No secondary links to distract.

**Subject lines:** Both use the second person ("you") and contrast the alumni's success with someone else's need — emotionally engaging without being manipulative. A/B test to see which framing resonates more with the existing list.

**SEO note for email:** While email itself is not indexed by Google, the donation landing page linked from this email should target keywords like "SRH University scholarship fund" and "support students Germany" for any prospective donors who find it via search.

### Brand & SEO Notes — Newsletter 2

**Voice:** Warm but practical. The opening ("You did it.") mirrors the SRH brand's aspiration-first framing while immediately shifting to actionable orientation. This email is more about function than inspiration — new students are anxious, not dreamy.

**Tone dial:** On-brand, leaning slightly more conversational. This is a relationship-building email, not a conversion email.

**Messaging pillars used:** The CORE principle (explained in plain language, not jargon), practical support infrastructure (Career Service, International Office), community (peer connection).

**Structure:** Three numbered steps reduce cognitive load. A table for contacts removes the need to search — information density without visual complexity.

**Banned language avoided:** No "we are delighted to inform you," no "kindly note," no passive constructions. All sentences are direct and second-person.

**P.S. line:** The postscript is a deliberate technique — email studies consistently show P.S. lines have high read rates even among people who skim. Used here for a practical but time-sensitive prompt (accommodation) rather than the primary CTA.

**Subject lines:** Option A leads with the milestone ("You're in") and provides immediate utility. Option B is more personalised using the first name merge tag. A/B test on open rate — personalised subject lines typically perform well but the milestone framing may outperform for this segment.

**SEO note:** The email should link to a dedicated "New Student Checklist" landing page optimised for queries like "SRH University student portal login" and "SRH Welcome Week [year]" — useful for students who search for these after the email lands.

# Newsletter 3 — MBA Prospective Student Nurture

**Audience:** Prospective student who has enquired about the MBA programme but has not yet applied  
**Goal:** Move the prospect from consideration to application — provide information, reduce objections, reinforce value  
**Tone:** Confident, practical, direct — peer advisor not salesperson  
**CTA:** Apply Now / Book a call with the study advisor

---

**Subject Line A:** The MBA questions most people are too afraid to ask — answered.  
**Subject Line B:** Still thinking about the MBA? Here is what you actually need to know.

**Preview text:** Cost, career outcomes, entry requirements — everything in one place.

Dear [First Name],

You have been thinking about the MBA. You have probably also been asking yourself some version of the same questions that everyone asks — and that nobody puts in the brochure.

Is it worth the cost? Will it actually change my career? Do I have the right background to get in? Can I manage it alongside work or other commitments?

These are the right questions. Here are the straight answers.

---

**"Is the SRH MBA worth the investment?"**

The SRH MBA is €1,300 per month for EU/EEA students over two semesters — one year in total. That is approximately €15,600 for the full programme.

Graduates from this programme move into roles including General Manager, Marketing Director, Head of Business Development, and Management Consultant. Many report salary increases within 12 months of graduating.

We will not give you a number we cannot stand behind. What we can tell you is that this programme is built to produce people who are ready to lead — not people who are ready to learn how to lead in theory.

---

**"What does the programme actually look like day to day?"**

The MBA is full-time, based in Berlin, and taught entirely in English. It runs across two semesters:

**Semester 1** — Strategy, Analysis, Human Factors, Data & Digital, Practice  
**Semester 2** — Marketing, Process & Quality, Team & Collaboration, Master's Thesis

Classes are built on the CORE principle: small groups, real case studies, practical projects. You will not spend your time in lectures. You will spend your time solving problems — with professors who have worked in the industries they teach.

---

**"Do I qualify to apply?"**

The requirements are:

- A completed Bachelor's degree (any discipline)
- Minimum 2 years of managerial or leadership experience
- English language proficiency (IELTS, TOEFL, or equivalent — or proof of English-medium prior study)
- A letter of motivation
- Your CV
- A copy of your ID or passport

If you have managed people, budgets, or projects — you likely qualify. The programme is designed for professionals who are ready to move up, not professionals who are just starting out.

---

**"What happens after I apply?"**

The process is straightforward:

1. Submit your application online — it takes around 20 minutes
2. If you meet the requirements, you will be invited to a personal interview (in-person or by phone)
3. If the interview is successful, you receive your acceptance letter and study contract
4. Sign the contract, pay the enrolment fee, and you are in
5. Join Welcome Week at the start of your semester

Applications are accepted on a rolling basis. The next intake starts **[date]**. Places are limited.

### Brand & SEO Notes — Newsletter 3

**Voice:** Direct and transparent. This email takes the voice of an informed advisor who gives real answers rather than a recruiter who deflects difficult questions. This is the SRH brand principle of "what you need to know, not what sounds good."

**Tone dial:** Confident and informative — sitting at "On brand" with a lean toward practical. The audience is an adult professional making a significant financial decision. They want clarity, not enthusiasm.

**Structure using objection framing:** The email is structured around four real questions a prospective student would ask. This mirrors a search engine optimisation approach — content that answers specific questions ranks well and converts better than content that promotes. Each question is a likely search query ("is an MBA worth the investment," "MBA entry requirements Germany").

**Transparency on cost:** The exact cost (€15,600 for EU/EEA students) is stated explicitly. This is both a brand decision (SRH's brand guidelines emphasise transparency over softening) and an SEO signal — cost pages and cost-transparent content are among the highest-converting in HE marketing.

**Named signatory:** The email is signed by a real, named study advisor (Beatriz Terol, as featured on the SRH website) rather than an anonymous "SRH Team." This is an E-E-A-T signal — it increases trust and reply rates. It also connects the email experience to the website experience.

**Dual CTA:** Unlike the other newsletters, this one has two CTAs — Book a Call (lower commitment) and Apply Now (higher commitment). This is intentional for the nurture stage: not everyone is ready to apply, but everyone can book a call. The lower-commitment CTA captures prospects who would otherwise disengage.

**Messaging pillars used:** Career transformation (specific roles named), CORE principle (explained functionally), transparency (exact costs and requirements), Berlin location (implicit — "based in Berlin, taught in English").

**Subject lines:** Option A uses curiosity and mild provocation ("questions most people are too afraid to ask"). Option B is direct and continuity-based ("Still thinking about...") — references the prior interaction without being presumptuous. A/B test based on list segment: Option B suits warmer leads who recently enquired; Option A suits colder leads who enquired weeks ago.

**SEO note:** This email's content — particularly the FAQ-style sections on cost, requirements, and the application process — maps directly to the highest-traffic informational queries for MBA programmes. These sections should also exist as standalone content on the website, structured with FAQPage schema markup.
